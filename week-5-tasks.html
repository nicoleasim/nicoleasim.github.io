    Task 2: Evaluate the Use of Text

    The Science Museum

    Characteristcs Evaluation
    Accuracy The text is accurate
    Active Voice There is no first person/reference to who is writing the website
    Bias There are no biases in particular. The museum is clearly aimed at children however the website is clarly catered for adults who are visiting or taking their children there. While this is obvious to me, there are no restrictions/phrases that suggest adults cannot also enjoy the exhibits.
    Clarity I have not noticed any technically difficult words.
    Conciseness The information is concise.
    Conversational While the language is formal they use ‘us’, and ‘ours’ when speaking about the museum and ‘you’ when speaking about the visitor. This allows for a smooth, conversational tone.
    Parallelism Yes there is parallelism in different areas of the website. For example when discussing opening hours of different exhibits they use the same words, giving a sense of unity.
    Positive Voice “If you have booked a ticket to visit our IMAX cinema, please ensure that you have also booked a free museum admission timeslot before arrival.” o In this example, the museum could have rephrased it to say that people without a museum timeslot would be denied entry, however that would have suggested restrictions and a missed opportunity.
    Strong Nouns/Verbs “The museum is open daily from 10.00–18.00 (last entry 17.15).” They could have phrased this as “The opening time for the museum is 10am and the closing time is 6pm…”, or something similar, which would have been quite clunky to read.
    Sentence Variety In the ‘Objects and Stories’ category of the site they have 3 different sections to look at, each with a different description. The first one starts with ‘Take a look’, the second with ‘Find out’, and the final being ‘Discover’. These all mean more or less the same thing and they essentially could have used the same one for each but it would have been too repetitive.
    Simple Words The website makes use of simple words and phrases.
    Shorter Paragraphs The website makes use of short paragraphs and sentences throughout the site. For example, they have a brief sentence describing the digital games underneath a still image of the game.
    Tone and Voice As the website is the official site for the museum, a formal tone is used to address the audience. It presents facts and information about the website rather than personality.

    Task 2: Samples for narrative

    VR: The game changer in humanities education
    As educators, we have the tools in place to change the way our children think. Enter the game-changer: Virtual Reality technology. Stepping beyond the traditional ways of learning, we can transform the experience of our children.
    The use of VR technology can engage children in humanities-based topics in brand-new ways. Imagine stepping foot into a WW2 scene, where children can merge their learning with firsthand ‘experience’.
    We are committed to exploring cost-effective VR resources with funding opportunities to make the use of VR a reality in the classroom. We encourage educators to share this point further afield, and to cease opportunities to use this equipment.
    Together, we can inspire a new generation of thinkers and storytellers, who have been able to see the world through different lenses through VR technology.

    Introducing a New Generation of Thinkers: Gaming in Humanities
    Our children are living in an increasingly digital society, so why not give them the tools to use it to their advantage? This website is an exploration of the integration of digital gaming in humanities education. The use of digital games in humanities education can beneficially affect children’s learning. From virtual reality technology to mobile apps, children can engage in new experiences that will transform their learning experiences.
    Implementing digital gaming into the classroom can reshape the experience of children learning subjects such as history. VR technologies can transport students to historical landscapes where they can be immersed into it, encouraging engagement and enthusiasm to learn.
    We are inviting educators to grasp the potential digital technologies can have in humanities education. We invite teachers to think about their own experiences using digital technologies and delve into how it affects their skills versus traditional methods of learning. Together we can create a generation of exceptional critical thinkers.

    Analysis

    • I have tried to use a passive voice. For example, instead of saying the possible negatives of not implementing digital technologies, I have focused on the positives they could bring.
    • I have not included any technical jargon. I understand that some teachers may not be aware of what I am talking about, and so have avoided using anything too specialized.
    • I have used a slightly convincing/demanding tone, but not in a way that my audience would be put off from it. I want to feel ‘togetherness’, and therefore have used ‘we’, quite often, as I want my audience to feel that I am one of them.
