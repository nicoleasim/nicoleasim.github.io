    Task 1

    I chose to compare the Yale School of Art and the Design Museums websites, in terms of design thinking.

    Design Museum

    • In terms of the audience, the Design Museum have clearly developed a website that is created for their audience. It would be appropriate to assume that the website is aimed at first time or regular visitors, as the homepage says 'Welcome to the Design Museum', and gives the option to visit, become a member, donate or to visit the online shop. The design is sleek, and is in line with those who would likely be interested in the Design Museum.
    • The purpose of the website is clearly to alert visitors of the site to 'What is on'. It is a very simple to use website, with a drop down menu of all the areas you can navigate to. The search bar however is not available on the home page and you need to scroll to the bottom when you click menu, which could be frustrating for users who are looking for something specific in a rush.
    • When you click one of the options e.g. ‘Plan your visit’, the title is at the top of the page so you know where you are. From there, you can either go back to ‘Home’, or click the drop down menu once again but it does not show where you currently are when you click the drop down menu so users have the possibility to click where they are again.
    • For the most part, it is stagnant images on the website. I like how for the background on some pages they have a full-length photo that stays still, and when you scroll the text moves while the image stays the same. This makes the text stand out sufficiently. I feel like it makes the using of the website a sleek experience, as although images and colours may change there is still a level of consistency over each section.
    • For those that are not regular visitors or are vsiting for the first time it is easy to navigate and it has a good amount of content that would be relevant. For people who are regular visitors, the content is still good however they may want a more personalised experience, however they can get this from being a member of the museum

    Yale School of Art

    • In terms of audience, it is not very clear if the Yale School of Art is acccurately aimed at its audience. On one hand, the wiki aspect of the website would suggest it was aimed at students as there is the feature of collaboration, however there is information regarding events and such that are not solely aimed at students, and the design of the website might not be appealing to the average person.
    • The animation and changing colours of the website is very jarring to a user. Although this may be appealing to students or prospective students, as the collaboration aspect is in line with an art school, it is difficult to navigate and actually want to read. There is an option however to 'turn off' the animations.
    • There is a lack of consistency over the entire website. Each section can be similar but also entirely different - this can make it difficult for a user to navigate and acrually make use of the site.
    • It has a good depth of material that is appropriate for the audience, with different sections that are only for students vs sections for visitors. It does have a good, clear calendar that details events.
