    Task 1: Tools for Web Accessibility

    The Science Museum

    The readability test results for this website displayed a score of 62.4. This implies that the content is easily understandable for individuals aged 13 to 14 and beyond. Considering the target audience for the Science Museum, which includes school-aged children, parents, and teachers, this aligns seamlessly with the intended readership. As I've discussed previously, the website caters to those who are either planning a visit to the museum or utilising its online resources.
    The website analysis revealed 11 critical errors that demand immediate attention for optimal user experience. One prevalent issue is the absence of alternative text for linked images. These images, representing screenshots of interactive games available on the site, lack descriptive text for users relying on screen readers. This oversight compromises accessibility and inhibits users from understanding the content and functionality of the images.
    Fixing the lack of alternative text should be a priority for the websiteâ€™s enhancement. Through doing this, they can ensure that individuals using screen readers can receive a similar experience to those who do not need them. Particularly as the images are interactive games that a user may be unable to play, alternative texts can enhance the users' experience and provide some sort of value for them.
